Introduction

What is cyberbullying?

Cyberbullying is any form of bullying that happens through digital devices and online platforms. It includes hurtful messages, spreading rumors, sharing private photos without permission, impersonation, threats and creating fake accounts to humiliate someone. Because it happens online, attackers can stay anonymous and the content spreads fast.

Effects of cyberbullying

  • Anxiety, persistent fear and stress
  • Lowered self-esteem and social withdrawal
  • Problems concentrating at school or work
  • Emotional issues such as sadness or depression
  • Long-term avoidance of online spaces

Why report the problem?

Reporting can stop the abuse, help platforms remove harmful accounts, protect others, and enable adults or authorities to intervene when necessary. Ignoring the issue often makes it worse.

Protect your personal information

Tips to prevent personal data from being used to manipulate or humiliate you:

  • Use privacy settings and make accounts private
  • Avoid posting sensitive details like address or phone number
  • Use strong passwords and two-factor authentication
  • Don't share intimate photos — they can be distributed
  • Be cautious with friend requests from people you don't know

How can I stop cyberbullying?

  1. Don't respond aggressively — it often escalates the situation
  2. Take screenshots as evidence
  3. Block and report the bully on the platform
  4. Talk to a trusted adult: parent, teacher or counselor
  5. If threats are serious, contact the authorities

If you see someone else being bullied, be an upstander: report, offer support and encourage the person to seek help.
